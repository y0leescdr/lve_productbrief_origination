Land Viability Engine (LVE)

Technical Product Brief for Origination Teams

Technology Overview

Executive Summary

LVE is an automated geospatial screening platform designed to accelerate the "First Pass" assessment of renewable energy sites. It replaces manual Google Earth verification with an instant, standardized audit of physical constraints.

Core Function: Instantly qualifies or disqualifies a site based on 4 non-negotiable physical criteria (Slope, Access, Land Use, Grid).

Live System View Zone ID: 3396f77c

The 4-Point Screening Standard

Slope Analysis

Checks median slope against standardized constructability thresholds to flag high civil works risks.

Grid Proximity

Calculates distance to nearest viable substation or transmission corridor.

Land Use

Flags non-viable zones such as built-up areas, water bodies, and wetlands.

Road Access

Verifies proximity to main roads for construction logistics and heavy equipment.

Why this works for Origination

Instead of requesting GIS analysis for every raw lead, the Origination team uses LVE to filter the "noise" instantly. This ensures your GIS and Engineering experts only spend time simulating sites that have already passed the physical viability threshold.
